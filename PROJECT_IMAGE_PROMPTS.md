## 4. Rain Barrel System — Water · Easy · 2–3 hr · €40–100

**Overview.** 1 mm rain on 1 m² roof = 1 L water. 200 L barrel = 200 kg full — platform must be solid. Mesh screen non-negotiable for mosquitoes.
**Materials.** 200 L food-grade barrel, downpipe diverter, tap, 2 m overflow hose, 4 cinder blocks, mesh screen.
**Method (5 stages).** Platform → diverter → tap → screen → overflow.

### Step 1 — Cinder-block platform, level
> 3D oblique view of four concrete cinder blocks stacked two-high in a 2 × 2 arrangement next to a house wall and downpipe, a paving slab underneath them on soft ground. A spirit level resting on top of the blocks shows perfectly level. Empty barrel space above implied.

### Step 2 — Cut downpipe + install diverter
> 3D close-up of a vertical white plastic downpipe attached to a brick wall. A hacksaw mid-cut at barrel-top height. A diverter fitting installed angling out toward where the barrel will sit. Pipe continues downward to the original drain below the diverter.

### Step 3 — Drill tap 5 cm from bottom
> 3D cutaway detail of the lower barrel wall: a brass tap with rubber washer on the inside and outside, drilled 5 cm above the bottom of the barrel (not at the absolute bottom, so sediment settles below the outlet). Silicone sealant bead visible around the joint. Dimension callout "5 cm".

### Step 4 — Mesh screen on lid
> 3D top-down view of the closed barrel lid showing the downpipe entry hole covered with fine stainless mesh screen secured by a rubber gasket. Cross-hatched mesh pattern clearly drawn. A small mosquito icon shown blocked from entering.

## 8. Drip Irrigation Line (per 20 m) — Water · Easy · 2–4 hr · €30–80

**Overview.** Drip + mulch cuts water use 50–70 % vs sprinkler. €30 timer pays for itself in one season. Pressure reducer is non-negotiable — mains pressure blows emitters off.
**Materials.** 20 m main hose 16 mm, 30 emitters (2 L/hr), 4 T-connectors, 4 end caps, battery timer, pressure reducer, tap connector, inline filter.
**Method (6 stages).** Connect → lay hose → punch emitters → cap ends → timer → test.

### Step 3 — Punch + push in emitters

## How to use these prompts in ChatGPT

1. Pick a project + step.
2. Paste the **Global style prompt** first, then the step prompt, into ChatGPT image generation.
3. For a consistent set, generate all steps of one project in one session — ChatGPT carries style continuity across sequential image requests.
4. To force exact dimensions onto the image, append: *"Render the labels exactly as given, no extra text."*
5. To keep the diagram aesthetic but in 3D, ask for: *"isometric clay-render, low-poly, no people unless specified."*
6. For a book/manual layout, request: *"square 1:1 frame, room for caption below, neutral cream background #f5f1e8."*
